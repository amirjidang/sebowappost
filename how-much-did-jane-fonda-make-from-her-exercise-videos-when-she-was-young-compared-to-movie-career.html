How Much Did Jane Fonda Earn From Her Workout Videos?

Jane Fonda's workout videos helped to popularize aerobics in the 1980s and made her a household name. She was one of the first celebrities to capitalize on the home video market, and her success helped to pave the way for other fitness gurus, such as Richard Simmons and Billy Blanks.

Fonda has earned an estimated $100 million from her workout videos. Her first workout video, Jane Fonda's Workout, hit the shelves in 1982 and became a sensation, selling over 17 million copies. To this day, it holds the record as the best-selling home exercise video of all time.

Throughout her career, Fonda has released a total of 22 workout videos, collectively selling 16 million copies. In addition to her videos, she has written five books on fitness, which have sold a total of 10 million copies.

Even today, Fonda's workout videos continue to enjoy popularity. People of all ages and fitness levels utilize her videos as a means to get in shape and stay fit. She has embraced the digital age and tapped into social media platforms to connect with a new wave of supporters. Through TikTok, Fonda shares the revival of her iconic exercise series and imparts her fitness wisdom to a new generation in prioritizing their well-being.

Fonda remains dedicated to her own fitness regimen and serves as a vocal advocate for healthy living, inspiring others to embrace a wholesome lifestyle.

Jane Fonda's Workout Videos Are Geared Towards Her Activism And Mental Health

Jane Fonda's Workout, released in 1982, revolutionized the fitness industry and became the best-selling home video of its time. "I had entered my so-called adult life at a time when challenging physical exercise was not offered to women. We weren’t supposed to sweat or have muscles," she wrote in My Life So Far.

Since the 1980s, Fonda has been dedicated to fitness. Her initial home video proved immensely successful, although the profits were directed toward a cause she deeply cared about. According to Vanity Fair, Fonda aimed to generate enough funds to support the California campaign for economic democracy, aligning her business venture with her activism. The timing proved fortuitous, as the workout video met the demands of the audience.

Fonda has long been involved in civil rights activism, consistently raising her voice for causes she believes in. In the 1970s, she stirred controversy by expressing her disapproval of the Vietnam War, notably posing in an anti-aircraft gunner's seat in Vietnam, which generated a backlash for allegedly snitching on the US. While she later expressed remorse for her actions, Fonda's dedication to activism remains unwavering. She has spoken out against the Iraq War, and the Dakota Access Pipeline, and has served as a Goodwill Ambassador for the United Nations Population Fund.

In recent years, Fonda has continued her activism, focusing on climate change. She participated in protests and was arrested for her convictions. The COVID-19 pandemic altered the way she engaged in in-person protests, but she adapted by organizing virtual Fire Drill Fridays in collaboration with Greenpeace, a non-profit organization. Fonda's commitment to spreading awareness about climate change led her to release the book, What Can I Do? in 2020, with all net proceeds going to Greenpeace.

On a personal level, Fonda's workout regime had a positive impact, especially in her battle with bulimia. The fitness guru has a share of her health issues, but the exercises provided her with a sense of control over her body, contributing to her recovery. "I was doing the workout before I started the business, and it gave me back a sense of control over my body," Fonda told People. "I started the workout, and that kind of cemented my ability to eat normal, which I can do now. Some people say you can never get over it, but you can."

How Much Did Jane Fonda Earn From Her Movie Career?

Jane Fonda's acting career has been a remarkable success, leading her to accumulate an estimated net worth of over $200 million. Her immense talent has garnered her two Academy Awards, two Golden Globe Awards, and a Tony Award, while also earning her nominations for esteemed accolades like the Emmy and Screen Actors Guild awards.

Fonda has amassed an estimated $100 million from her film career. Among her highest-grossing films are Klute ($100 million), Coming Home ($120 million), and On Golden Pond ($130 million). She also made $1 million for her role in The Electric Horseman, $2 million for 9 to 5 with Dolly Parton and Lily Tomlin, and over $3 million for Stanley & Iris as reported by Wealthy Persons. These substantial figures reflect her status as one of the highest-paid actresses in the industry.

Furthermore, her television endeavors have contributed approximately $50 million to her overall wealth. Notably, her highly-rated show Grace and Frankie drew an average of 10 million viewers per episode, showcasing her enduring appeal and popularity.
