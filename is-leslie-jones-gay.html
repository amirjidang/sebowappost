Leslie Jones’ Rise To Fame

In 1987, Jones started performing stand-up comedy in college after one of her friends entered her in a “Funniest Person on Campus” competition. Jones quit school and moved to Los Angeles after winning the competition, where she has been working since.

She was introduced to several of “the top managers in comedy” in 2012 after Chris Rock saw her perform, but they all “didn’t understand it,” according to Chris Rock. The next year, Rock assisted Jones in getting a Saturday Night Live audition, which she successfully completed.

Jones participated in Katt Williams’ It’s Pimpin’ Pimpin’ tour in 2008.

In the action comedy “National Security” (2003) and the political thriller “The Company We Keep,” she is best recognized for playing Lay La and Beverly Blue, respectively, in 2010’s.

In “Ghostbusters,” Leslie portrayed Patty Tolan in 2016. Yet the amount of misogyny hurled against the film was astounding.

Despite claims made by many men online that an all-female “Ghostbusters” movie would ruin their childhoods, the movie brought in $229.1 million at the box office.

Jones was also signed by Netflix for a 2020 stand-up special.

She has received multiple honors, including the People’s Choice Award for Favorite Comedy Act in 2020 and the MTV Movie Award for Best Comedic Performance in 2021.

Leslie Jones In The Olympics

Jones continued to perform her duties at the 2018 Winter Olympics in South Korea and again live-tweeted the 2020 Summer Olympics that were postponed to Tokyo, Japan. For the last time, she tweeted live during the 2022 Winter Olympics in Beijing, China.

She claimed that NBC, the broadcaster, was forcing her to stop live-tweeting and that this would be the last Olympics she would do so.
