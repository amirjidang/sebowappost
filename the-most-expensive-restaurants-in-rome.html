Expensive restaurants have always been a tourist attraction in Rome. The capital city of Italy is not an exception when it comes to being home to some of the most expensive restaurants in the world. Rome has been not only a major gateway to Europe for thousands of years but also a major tourist attraction for all of that time. While a city like Rome is a great place to visit, there is also a thriving restaurant scene that can make even a short trip turn into a long vacation. This blog will list the top 8 most expensive restaurants in Rome, as of 2022.

La Pergola

The most expensive restaurant in Rome is La Pergola. Le Pergola is certainly a place of luxury, as it is the only restaurant in the area to have been awarded three Michelin stars. The menu is rather expensive, but the quality of the food makes it well worth the money. The atmosphere of the location is incredible, and while you're eating, you can look out over the city and enjoy the sights.

Il San Lorenzo

The elegant atmosphere and delectable fish at Il San Lorenzo have earned it a stellar reputation. This restaurant's ambiance and superb service are both contributing factors to its Michelin star status. Il San Lorenzo is a traditional roman restaurant located in the heart of Rome, in front of the Pantheon. Il San Lorenzo offers its guests typical Roman dishes and a taste of true Italian cuisine that you won't find anywhere else. Don't forget to select a bottle of wine from their large wine list to go along with your dinner.

Il Pagliaccio

Anthony Genovese, the executive chef at Il Pagliaccio, is a seasoned professional who has gained valuable experience through working in a variety of nations around the world. Il Pagliaccio is an absolute must-visit restaurant in Rome due to its huge wine list, which features 1300 different options to choose from. Il Pagliaccio is a restaurant in Rome that uses seasonal ingredients and traditional cooking techniques to create authentic Italian dishes. Their menu features the finest lamb, beef, and fish as well as classic Roman favorites.

La Terrasse

The stunning atmosphere of La Terrasse has earned it a great deal of reputation in and around Rome. On the very top floor of Hotel Eden is where you'll find the restaurant. Guests who choose to dine in can do so while taking in the breathtaking scenery. The place features large windows that reach from floor to ceiling, which makes it possible for fresh air to enter the building. The prices on the menu begin at $150, and La Terrasse is home to one of the most extensive wine collections in the area.

Antico Arco

Antico Arco is recognized as both one of the most expensive and one of the city's finest restaurants. Because their food is so well-liked by the patrons, the restaurant has been awarded a Michelin Plate. Antico Arco is known for having the most breathtaking vantage points, despite the fact that it is situated somewhat outside of the main part of the city.

Aroma

The spectacular rooftop view of the Colosseum that Aroma offers is quite well-known among both tourists and locals in Rome. The menu draws its inspiration from both modern cuisine of the Mediterranean and traditional Roman fare. Take this piece of advice: order the Ricciola fish with a coffee and sesame crust, served with a black olive and mango cream.

Glass Hostaria

It is highly recommended that you pay a visit to Glass Hostaria in order to get a feel for the authentic atmosphere of Rome. The seafood dishes at this restaurant are among the most well-known on the menu due to the presence of a Texas-born chef of international renown. Glass Hostaria restaurant is located in one of Rome's most important squares. Thanks to its beautiful view, it is a perfect place for those who want to spend an enjoyable evening in the city. You can choose from a wide range of innovative and refined dishes that are the result of the authentic fusion between Italian and Latin American cuisines.

Panificio Bonci

This is regarded as one of, if not the best restaurant in Rome. It is more of a landmark than a restaurant. It is a bit of a trek from the Vatican Museums, but the walk is well worth it for a quick, inventive supper after a tour of the Vatican. Panificio Bonci is named for its founder, Gabriele Bonci. It is referred to by Panificio as a bread shop, which is the literal foundation of good pizza. This location is a must if you have the time during your sightseeing to visit it. Here you will find otherworldly delights and damn amazing food.
