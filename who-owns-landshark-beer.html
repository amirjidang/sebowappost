Who Owns Landshark Beer? Landshark Beer Company History

August 2024 · 12 minute read

Landshark Beer, a popular brand known for its beach-themed aesthetic, is owned by Margaritaville Brewing Co. The beer was created in collaboration with Anheuser-Busch, who brews and distributes it. Landshark Lager was introduced in 2007 and quickly gained popularity, especially among fans of Jimmy Buffett and the laid-back beach culture he represents.

The origins of Landshark Beer can be traced back to a chance encounter between Buffett and his manager in 1984. Buffett’s desire for Corona as a sponsor led to a partnership between him and the beer brand. This partnership inadvertently resulted in the creation and success of Landshark Beer, as Corona incorporated the Margaritaville aesthetic into its promotional materials.

Landshark Beer’s success skyrocketed after it was embraced by Buffett’s fan base, known as Parrotheads. The beer became synonymous with the Margaritaville lifestyle and quickly became one of the top-selling beers in America. Margaritaville Brewing Co. owns the brand, but Anheuser-Busch handles the brewing and distribution, allowing Landshark Beer to reach a wide audience.

Despite mixed reviews from beer critics, Landshark Beer continues to be a favorite choice for those seeking a refreshing and laid-back beer experience. Its popularity can be attributed to its connection with Buffett’s music and the Margaritaville lifestyle. As the Margaritaville brand expands, Landshark Beer is likely to remain a key part of its portfolio.

The Origins of Landshark Beer

Landshark Beer traces its roots back to a fortuitous meeting between Jimmy Buffett, the renowned singer-songwriter, and his manager Howard Kaufman in 1984. During a conversation, Buffett expressed his desire to have Corona as a sponsor for his concerts and beach-themed brand, Margaritaville. This conversation sparked a partnership between Buffett and Corona, leading to the creation of Landshark Beer.

Corona, known for its association with beach culture, embraced the Margaritaville aesthetic in its advertisements and promotional materials, thanks to Buffett’s influence. This collaboration inadvertently paved the way for the birth and success of Landshark Beer, a brand that embodies the laid-back spirit of the Margaritaville lifestyle.

The Collaboration Between Jimmy Buffett and Corona

The partnership between Jimmy Buffett and Corona not only brought Landshark Beer to fruition but also brought together two iconic brands. Buffett’s influence on Corona’s marketing strategies allowed for the seamless integration of the Margaritaville beach lifestyle into Corona’s messaging.

Landshark Beer and Anheuser-Busch

Landshark Beer is owned by Margaritaville Brewing Co., but it is brewed by Anheuser-Busch. Margaritaville Brewing Co. licenses the Landshark Beer trademark to Anheuser-Busch, who handles the production and distribution. This partnership allows Landshark Beer to reach a wide audience and benefit from Anheuser-Busch’s brewing expertise and distribution network.

Despite being owned by Margaritaville Brewing Co., Landshark Beer is brewed and distributed by Anheuser-Busch, allowing it to leverage the resources and industry knowledge of one of the largest brewing companies in the world. Landshark Beer’s Manufacturing Process

Landshark Beer is crafted using standard brewing techniques that are typical for lagers. The primary production facility for Landshark Beer is located in St. Louis, Missouri, USA, where Anheuser-Busch operates one of its many breweries. This state-of-the-art facility is equipped with modern brewing equipment and adheres to strict quality control measures to ensure that every batch of Landshark Beer meets the brand’s high standards.

The brewing process begins with the selection of the finest ingredients, including malted barley, hops, water, and yeast. These ingredients are carefully combined and undergo a mashing process where enzymes convert the starches in the barley into sugars. This sugar-rich liquid, known as wort, is then transferred to fermentation tanks where yeast is added to initiate the fermentation process.

During fermentation, the yeast consumes the sugars in the wort and produces alcohol and carbon dioxide as byproducts. This process takes place at controlled temperatures for a specific period of time to achieve the desired flavor, aroma, and alcohol content. Once fermentation is complete, the beer undergoes a lagering phase where it is stored at near-freezing temperatures, enabling it to mature and develop its crisp and refreshing taste.

After the lagering phase, Landshark Beer is meticulously filtered to remove any remaining impurities, ensuring a clean and clear appearance. The beer is then carbonated to achieve the desired level of effervescence. Finally, it is packaged in bottles, cans, and kegs, ready to be enjoyed by beer enthusiasts across the country.

With meticulous attention to detail and a commitment to quality, Landshark Beer delivers an authentic island-style lager experience that captures the essence of the Margaritaville lifestyle. From the brewing process to the final product, Landshark Beer embodies the spirit of relaxation, fun, and laid-back enjoyment that Jimmy Buffett and the brand’s founder envisioned.
