Why NeNe Leakes Is Suing 'Real Housewives'

August 2024 · 5 minute read

NeNe Leakes, the former Real Housewives of Atlanta reality star is suing Bravo, Andy Cohen, NBC Universal, True Entertainment and Truly Original for racism and hostility in the workplace.

The 54-year-old previously spoke up about ex-co-star Kim Zolciak-Biermann’s racially motivated offenses and claimed she wasn’t allowed to talk about Black Lives Matter on the reality show. She believes the network encouraged racist behavior and fostered an uncomfortable work environment. NeNe Leakes, whose legal name is Linnethia Monique Leakes, spent seven seasons as one of the most popular stars of The Real Housewives of Atlanta between 2008 and 2020.

The popular former The Real Housewives of Atlanta cast member has just started legal proceedings against both the network and production companies responsible for the show. Andy Cohen is included in the accused parties, a shocking allegation as the pair have appeared to be close friends over their years working together.

So what is all this legal drama about, and why is this quick-witted, beloved star of the reality TV franchise suing the people behind it? We delve further into this lawsuit, what caused it and why NeNe Leakes is suing Andy Cohen and The Real Housewives TV franchise.

7 Did Bravo Encourage Racist Behavior?

The American TV personality has alleged that the network, production companies and executives involved not only allowed racist attitudes but encouraged this type of offensive behavior. As per court documents, NeNe Leakes has accused the show’s production teams of “[fostering] a corporate and workplace culture in which racially insensitive and inappropriate behaviour is tolerated – if not encouraged”.

6 Kim Zolciak-Biermann Mentioned In NeNe Leakes' Court Documents

NeNe Leakes' cast mate and on/off friend Kim Zolciak-Biermann is not being sued, but her name appears multiple times throughout the lawsuit.

Related: Does Kim Zolciak Speak To Any Of Her Former ‘RHOA’ Castmates?

Over their years working together on the reality show, Leakes has accused Zolciak-Biermann (and in one instance her daughter Brielle) of racially offensive behavior including comments made during the show’s production as well as online. The lawsuit claims that Leakes attempted to address these issues with the show’s executives but was unsuccessful.

5 NeNe Leakes Believes She Is Target Of Systematic Racism

NeNe Leakes’ lawyer David deRubertis explained that at the crux of the case was that “Nene was the target of systemic racism from co-star Kim Zolciak-Biermann, which was tolerated by Bravo executive producer Andy Cohen and other executives.”

Radar Online reported that NeNe Leakes exposed emails from the show’s executives saying that calling out the behavior of her fellow housewives would be “bad for business”.

Related: Here's How The Cast Of 'Real Housewives Of Atlanta' Feel About NeNe Leakes And Her Rumored Return

The lawsuit further also reveals that not only was Zolciak-Biermann’s behavior tolerated, it was even rewarded. “Nene says she complained to Andy Cohen and producers – who instead of firing Kim gave her a spin-off.”

4 Kim Made Racist Comments 'RHOA'

The paperwork obtained alleges that during the first season of the reality show, which aired in 2008, the cast was planning to attend a barbecue, and housewife Kim Zolciak-Biermann, responded to the idea with “words to the effect of: "I don’t want to sit around with NeNe and eat chicken.’”

The suit says the statement “perpetuated an offensive stereotype about African-Americans.”

In another incident, during the fifth season, Zolciak-Biermann made “racially offensive and stereotypical” comments about the new home of housewife Kandi Burruss, calling her neighborhood a “ghetto” and perpetuating a racial stereotype when asking whether Burruss needed to have a swimming pool.

3 Why NeNe Leakes Left 'RHOA'

Another significant complaint made in the lawsuit includes NeNe Leakes' revealing that the show's executives were partly responsible for her forced exit from the show in 2020. At the time, the Black Lives Matter movement was dominating the news, with Leakes being an outspoken and active supporter.

The show’s executives are accused of silencing Leakes and stopping her from addressing the BLM movement on the Bravo reality TV show. The lawsuit accuses the show’s production of violating American federal employment and anti-discrimination laws.

Related: Ratings For ‘RHOA’ Are Dropping Since Nene Leakes’ Departure, But Will Bravo Bring Her Back?

“As the Black Lives Matter movement swept our nation, Mrs. Leakes — Bravo’s historically most successful Black female talent — should have been embraced by NBC, Bravo, and True,” the suit says. “Instead, NBC, Bravo, and True forced her out of the ‘house she built,’ denying her a regular role.”

2 Andy Cohen and Nene Leakes Have Fractured Friendship

Things have been tense between NeNe Leakes and Andy Cohen for some time, so it's no surprise that he is mentioned in her lawsuit.

In an interview with Entertainment Tonight back in October 2020, she spoke about her relationship with Cohen, "Well, I thought that we had a relationship and a great relationship. But maybe we didn't. I don't know."

After announcing her departure from The Real Housewives of Atlanta earlier in 2020, she tweeted at Andy Cohen: "@Andy said he respected Teddi [Mellencamp] for telling the truth about her exit. Well i can’t wait to tell my truths i hope i get just as much respect." And after a fan asked on Twitter whether the network forced NeNe to leave, she responded with, “They definitely did.”

“Keep trying me sir and imma let the world know who you really are,” she later tweeted, which many people felt was directed at the host and producer. “The racist is the master manipulator! They using me for ratings like they have always done.”

1 How NeNe Leakes Feels About The Whole Thing

Another of NeNe Leakes’ attorneys, Joe Habachy, said via email that “not a day goes by that NeNe doesn’t wake up with an onslaught of overwhelming emotions as a direct result of these unfortunate and avoidable occurrences.”

This lawsuit was just filed on Wednesday, April 20, in a federal court in Atlanta, so we will be keeping our eyes open to see where it goes from here.
