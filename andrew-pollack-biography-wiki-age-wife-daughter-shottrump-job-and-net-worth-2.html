Andrew Pollack Biography | Wiki

Andrew  Pollack is an American Author school safety activist, He became known to viewers during a CNN interview with Donald Trump. His daughter Meadow Pollack who died when she was 18-year-old was killed in the Stoneman Douglas High School shooting on February 14, 2018.

Andrew is publicly acknowledged with helping to pass the “Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School Public Safety Act” (Florida Senate Bill: 7026). So far he has met with President Donald Trump and his cabinet, Florida governor Rick Scott, and many lawmakers.  He is actively fighting to increase more federal funding to increase school security in every school across the nation. Andrew believes that bulletproof glass, metal detectors, enhanced door lock systems, ID checkpoints, and “school marshals” can help prevent school shootings.

Daughter’s murder made Andrew Pollack ‘pro-Second Amendment’

Andrew Pollack, known as the outspoken father of one of the students killed during the Parkland, Florida, mass shooting, said Wednesday that he was never a big gun-rights supporter until he realized how defenseless his daughter was when she was gunned down at school last year.

“I was never such a pro-Second Amendment type of guy until this happened to my daughter and I saw how she became such a victim on that third floor and no one came in to help her,” Mr. Pollack, whose 18-year-old daughter Meadow was among the 17 students and staff killed by a gunman on Valentine’s Day at Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School, told Fox News host Martha MacCallum.

Mr. Pollack cited the recent mass shooting at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas, where 22 people were killed and others attempted to hide or flee, as an example of people needing to be able to carry guns so they can fight back.
