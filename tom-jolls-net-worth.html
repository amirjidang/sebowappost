A glimpse into Tom Jolls career

Tom Jolls was born on August 16, 1933, in Newfane, New York, and was raised in Lockport, New York. 

He started his television journey by appearing at WBES-TV during its three short months of operation in 1953. 

Later he relocated to Buffalo and joined WBEN (AM) radio. 

He hosted the famous American show “The Superman Show,” where he worked himself as Captain Tom, who later became known as Commander Tom. 

These segments were aired in conjunction with Adventures of Superman, eventually leading to the development of The Commander Tom Show, which remained on the air in various formats for an impressive 26 years.

Furthermore, he also hosted “The Weather Outside,” where he used to report on weather conditions. 

His unique struggles with maintaining balance in gusty winds became a familiar sight to viewers.

Although he did not complete an official certification in Meteorology, still he firmly believed in the art of weather forecasting and maintained a subscription to Accuweather. 

After that, Tom Jolls served his duty as children’s host Commander Tom for the weekend morning kids show and received much love and recognition worldwide. 

The significant contributions of Tom Jolls, Irv Weinstein, and Rick Azar, were inducted into the Buffalo Broadcasting Hall of Fame in 1998. 

Moreover, he received the honor of induction into the New York State Broadcasters Association Hall of Fame in 2019, further solidifying his impact on the broadcasting industry.

Apart from his professional career, Tom Jolls was a devoted family man and fathered six children. 

The names of his children were Suzanne Marie, Thomas Dale, Kathleen Ann, Lisa Ann, Timothy Wilbert, and Terrence Leo.

As per sources, he resided in the town of Newfane in New York at the time of his death.

Tom Jolls dies at 89

On Wednesday, June 7, 2023, Tom Jolls departed from this world at the age of 89. He passed away at the Buffalo Hospice in Cheektowaga. 

He gained popularity after joining the famous WKBW-TV (Channel 7) anchor team with Irv Weinstein and Rick Azar. 

Jolls was the last member of the well-known trio to retire and the only one who chose to stay in Western New York even after a brief period of living in Florida. 

Moreover, his partners, Irv Weinstein, left this world in 2017, and Rick Azar in 2021.
