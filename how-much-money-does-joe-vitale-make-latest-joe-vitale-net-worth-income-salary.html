Joseph F. Vitale (born November 10, 1954) is an American politician who represents the 19th Legislative District in the New Jersey State Senate. He was elected to the Senate in 1998 and has been a member of the Democratic Party since since. His other notable accomplishments include serving as Mayor of Woodbridge Township in New Jersey, when he was chosen by the Township Council in July 2006 to fill a vacancy created by the death of previous Mayor Frank Pelzman.

 Senator Vitale was elected to the Senate in 1998, filling a vacancy caused when Jim McGreevey resigned from his position as part of his failed campaign for Governor of New Jersey in 1997. Senator Vitale has served in the Senate since 1998. From 2004 until 2009, Senator Vitale served as the Senate’s Deputy Majority Leader. Current positions include Chairman of the Senate Committee on Health, Human Services, and Senior Citizens as well as Vice Chairman of the Senate Committee on Economic Growth and Development. Vitale and William Gormley were the primary supporters of the New Jersey Homeless Youth Act, which was passed in 1999 and enables adolescents to seek homeless shelter without obtaining permission from their parents. 

He is also the author of legislation that would enable needle exchange programmes for drug users and that would ban people convicted of domestic violence crimes from owning firearms. The Senator was the primary sponsor of over forty bills that were signed into law, including legislation establishing the KidCare and FamilyCare health insurance programmes, legislation requiring nursing aides to undergo certified criminal background checks, legislation prohibiting mandatory overtime in health care facilities except in emergency situations, and legislation establishing the “New York Care Act.” Besides serving on the Board of Directors for the Middlesex County Habitat for Humanity, Senator Vitale also serves on the Visiting Nurses Association of Central Jersey’s Board of Trustees. Woodbridge Township’s John F. Kennedy Memorial High School was where Vitale went to high school.
